About Me

Biography

Mahmudul Haque is a machine learning enthusiast with a passion for exploring the frontiers of computer science. Mahmudul's journey began at the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST) where he pursued a Bachelor's degree in Electrical, Electronic and Communication Engineering (EECE). From there, he went on to work as an Adjunct Faculty and Research Assistant at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Independent University of Bangladesh (IUB) in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Mahmudul's expertise lies in the realm of computer visiona and robotics. He has authored several journal and conference papers, and his thesis, "Detection and Classification of Sensitive Audio-Visual Content for Automated Film Censorship and Rating," is a testament to his innovative thinking. His current projects include developing a pelvic bone segmentation and fracture detection system using radiographic images in collaboration with the University of Hyogo in Japan.

Aside from his technical prowess, Mahmudul is also a creative individual with a flair for design and video editing. He has honed his skills in various programming languages such as Python, C, C++, and MATLAB, and is proficient in image processing with OpenCV and machine learning with TensorFlow and Scikit-learn. He has also designed and developed apps using Flutter, built websites with Django and Flask, and leveraged cloud services on AWS.

In his free time, Mahmudul loves to engage in co-curricular activities and has won several awards, including the 2nd Runners-up position in the Line Following Robot Segment at Robolution 2019 and the Championship title in the Bangabandhu Inter-Department Cultural Competition 2021, where he led his team to victory.

Mahmudul's infectious curiosity and creative mindset make him an invaluable asset to any project, and he is always ready to tackle new challenges and push the boundaries of what is possible in the world of computer science.
